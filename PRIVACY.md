# Privacy

## What this tool does with data

Everything Paisley Ponytail recovers comes from the **Internet Archive's
Wayback Machine** — a public library of pages that were publicly visible on
Webshots between 1995 and 2012. The tool downloads to your machine and
nowhere else. There is no server, no account, no telemetry, no analytics,
and nothing is ever uploaded anywhere by this software.

`remarks.json` (your notes on screen names) lives next to where you run the
tool, stays on your machine, and is in `.gitignore` so it can't be committed
by accident.

## Intended use

Recovering **your own** photos, or those of family and friends who want them
back. Everything the tool can reach was public on the open web for years and
was preserved by a volunteer archival effort — but "publicly archived" and
"kind to republish" are different standards. Be a decent human with what you
recover.

## "I want photos of me removed from the archive"

This tool doesn't host anything, so I can't handle removal requests. The
Internet Archive can, and they have a real process for this:

- Email **info@archive.org** with the URLs (or describe the Webshots screen
  name/albums) and request exclusion from the Wayback Machine.
- Their policy: https://archive.org/about/faqs.php#Rights

Once the Archive excludes content, this tool can't recover it. There is no
other source.

## Private albums

Albums set to private on Webshots were never crawled and are not in the
archive. This tool can't reach them, and there's nothing to bypass.
